What is Financial Independence?

As teachers and small business owners, you are often told that you need to achieve financial independence in order to be successful. But what exactly is financial independence? And why is it so important?

In simple terms, financial independence means having enough income to support yourself without having to rely on others. This can mean different things to different people, but there are some commonalities. For example, most people who are financially independent have a healthy savings account, a strong credit score, and minimal debt.

You can achieve financial independence by following these same principles. By living below their means, saving regularly, and investing wisely, they can build the financial stability they need to thrive.

DEFINITION

"IN THE BLACK"

Being “in the black” is a phrase commonly used in accounting to describe financial health. If you are “in the black”, it means that you have a positive cash flow. In contrast, if you are “in the red”, you have a negative cash flow.
